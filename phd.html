PhD (Computer Science)

The highest academic degree awarded in an academic discipline

PhD in Computer Science Overview

PhD is the highest degree awarded in an academic discipline and is the most satisfying and rewarding educational experience. Students work closely with a faculty member, performing original research, and tackling challenging and unsolved problems.

The department offers PhD degrees with research emphasis on the following groups:

Human Information Interaction

This research group investigates all aspects of information usage by humans, with a focus on behavior, techniques, and storage models.

Contact Person: Dr. Shuaib Karim

skarim@qau.edu.pk

Knowledge Engineering

This group analyzes data and metadata using various algorithms, with focus on social computing, image processing, NLP, and more.

Contact Person: Dr. Onaiza Maqbool

oniaza@qau.edu.pk

Networking and Communication

This group covers applied aspects in networking, communication, IoT, next-gen networks, security, privacy, and much more.

Contact Person: Dr. Muazzam Khattak

muazzam.khattak@qau.edu.pk

PhD Courses Offered

  • CSC-851: Human Information Interaction

    Focuses on the study of human interaction with information systems, including information retrieval, behavior, and usability testing.

  • CSC-852: Information Architecture

    This course covers the principles of organizing and structuring information in a way that is intuitive and user-friendly for digital systems.

  • CSC-853: Collaborative Data Mining

    Analyzes methods for collaborative data mining and the use of multiple data sources to uncover hidden patterns and insights.

  • CSC-854: Enterprise Intelligence

    Focuses on the application of intelligence systems in enterprises for decision support, business analysis, and optimization.

  • CSC-855: Communication Networks

    This course covers the design and operation of communication networks, including wireless networks, protocols, and network security.

  • CSC-857: Software Evolution and Reengineering

    Explores the process of evolving software systems and refactoring legacy code to meet new requirements and improve functionality.

  • CSC-858: Program Comprehension and Reverse Engineering

    Techniques for understanding and analyzing existing software systems, including reverse engineering and program comprehension strategies.

  • CSC-861: Information Visualization and Presentation

    Teaches methods and tools for visualizing complex data, with a focus on effective presentation for decision-making and analysis.

  • CSC-862: Accessibility of Interactive Systems

    Examines the design and development of accessible user interfaces for people with disabilities, ensuring that interactive systems are inclusive.

  • CSC-863: Metadata Model Management

    Focuses on the management and integration of metadata models in large-scale information systems for efficient data storage and retrieval.

  • CSC-865: Advances in Next Generation Networks

    Explores emerging technologies in next-generation networks, including 5G, IoT, and their impact on data communication and systems architecture.

  • CSC-866: P2P-based Information Retrieval

    Examines the use of peer-to-peer (P2P) systems for information retrieval, including decentralized search and file-sharing protocols.

  • CSC-867: Advanced Software Architecture

    Focuses on advanced principles in software architecture design, covering scalable, maintainable, and secure software architectures.

  • CSC-868: Software Refactoring

    Techniques for improving the internal structure of existing software code without changing its external behavior, focusing on performance and maintainability.

  • CSC-869: Advanced Topics in Machine Learning

    Explores advanced machine learning topics, including reinforcement learning, unsupervised learning, and novel approaches in deep learning.

  • CSC-871: Digital Preservation

    Introduces methods for preserving digital information, including long-term storage solutions and strategies for maintaining digital content integrity.

  • CSC-872: Mining Massive Datasets

    Focuses on techniques for mining and analyzing large-scale datasets, covering parallel processing, distributed systems, and big data tools.

  • CSC-873: Ontology Engineering

    This course introduces the design and implementation of ontologies for knowledge representation in semantic web technologies.

  • CSC-874: Advanced Topics in Networking

    Covers advanced networking topics, including network security, routing algorithms, and the evolution of internet technologies.

  • CSC-875: Online Social Networking Systems: Technological Perspective

    Focuses on the technology behind online social networks, including user management, data storage, and real-time interactions.

  • CSC-876: Social Media Content Analysis

    Teaches techniques for analyzing content on social media platforms, including sentiment analysis, topic modeling, and network analysis.

  • CSC-877: Software Repositories Mining

    This course explores mining techniques for software repositories to identify trends, detect defects, and improve software quality.

  • CSC-878: Grammatical Inference for Software Engineering

    Introduces grammatical inference techniques to model software behavior and derive software specifications.

  • CSC-879: Software and System Specification

    Covers the specification techniques for both software systems and complex computing infrastructures to ensure quality and correctness.

  • CSC-881: Visual Analytics

    Focuses on integrating data visualization with analytics techniques to support better decision-making and data-driven insights.

  • CSC-885: Deep Learning on Graphs

    This course covers the application of deep learning techniques to graph-structured data, with a focus on graph neural networks and their use in various domains.
