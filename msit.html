MS (Information Science & Technology)

Evening programme for professionals

Program Overview

In today's Information Society, expertise in all forms of information is required for progress in science, business, and education. The main aim of the MS programme in Information Science & Technology (IST) is to design and develop information-related applications using information technology by understanding the relationship between digital information, people, and technology.

Program Structure

  • 24 credit hours for the course work and 6 credit hours for project/thesis
  • 30 credit hours for the course work

Eligibility

Students must have completed 16 years of education, either BS (4 years) or MSc in computing-related disciplines.

Courses

Compulsory Courses

  • IST-611: Human and Information Interaction

    Overview of course and a brief history; Types and structures of information resources, user modeling, and advanced searching.

  • IST-631: Web Services

    Introduction to web technology, markup languages, and the development frameworks for building web services.

Optional Courses

  • IST-620: Trends in Information Resources

    Technologies & documentary practices; Web spheres, Blogospheres, and the circulation of value in information networks.

  • IST-621: Designing Usable Information Services

    Analysis of traditional and digital information services, designing usable services, and user-centered service development.

  • IST-632: Advanced Web Development

    Learn the skills to build and deploy modern, responsive web applications, covering advanced technologies and frameworks.

  • IST-650: Web Science

    Explores the interdisciplinary nature of web science, including the structure, dynamics, and behavior of the web.

  • IST-652: Social and Organizational Issues of Information

    Focuses on the impact of information on organizations and society, including ethics, privacy, and the digital divide.

  • IST-655: Economics and Business in the Web

    Analyzes the economic and business implications of the web, focusing on e-commerce, digital marketing, and business models.

  • IST-660: Research Methods

    Teaches research methodologies, including data collection, analysis, and reporting, essential for graduate research projects.

  • IST-665: Cloud Computing

    Introduces cloud computing platforms and technologies, focusing on infrastructure as a service (IaaS) and software as a service (SaaS).

  • IST-667: Wireless Sensor Networks

    Explores the design, deployment, and management of wireless sensor networks, focusing on applications and protocols.

  • IST-670: Evaluation of Information Services

    Focuses on the methods for evaluating the effectiveness and usability of information systems and services.

  • IST-672: e-Government

    Examines the role of information technology in government services, focusing on the transformation to digital governance.

  • IST-673: Multimedia Technology (CS-673)

    Covers technologies in multimedia systems, including image, video, audio, and interactive technologies for modern applications.

  • IST-675: Information Retrieval Systems (CS-675)

    Introduction to information retrieval systems, focusing on search engines, indexing, and relevance ranking techniques.

  • IST-677: World Wide Web and Society (CS-677)

    Explores the social and cultural impact of the web, including online communities, digital inclusion, and privacy issues.

  • IST-773: Semantic Web (CS-773)

    Introduces the semantic web technologies for developing linked data, including RDF, OWL, SPARQL, and ontology modeling.

  • IST-774: HCI Models (CS-774)

    Focuses on human-computer interaction theories, models, and methodologies for designing usable and accessible interfaces.

  • IST-775: Digital Libraries (CS-775)

    Explores the design, development, and management of digital libraries, including metadata standards and content management.

  • IST-776: Enterprise Architecture (CS-776)

    Introduces principles and practices of enterprise architecture, focusing on the integration of information systems and business processes.

  • IST-778: Ontology Design and Development (CS-778)

    Explores the design and development of ontologies for knowledge representation, including the use of ontologies in the semantic web.

  • IST-785: Selected Topics in Information Science & Technology

    Covers advanced and emerging topics in information science, allowing flexibility for specialized study.

  • IST-781: Content-based Information Retrieval

    Focuses on information retrieval based on content, including text, image, and multimedia data analysis techniques.

  • IST-815: Neural Information Retrieval

    Introduces the use of neural networks in information retrieval, including deep learning methods for search systems.

  • IST-821: Design for Usability (CS-821)

    Focuses on user-centered design principles and practices for creating accessible, usable, and effective interfaces.

  • IST-822: Modeling of Web Information Systems (CS-822)

    Covers the modeling and design of web-based information systems, focusing on architecture, scalability, and performance.
