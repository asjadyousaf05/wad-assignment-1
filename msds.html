MS (Data Science)

Evening Programme at Quaid-e-Azam University

MS (Data Science) Overview

Data Science has become essential due to the need for analyzing and understanding vast amounts of data generated by various sources. This programme will equip students with the necessary skills in programming, data analysis, big data analytics, and visualization to effectively work in local and international markets or pursue a PhD degree.

Part-Time MS (Data Science)

Quaid-i-Azam University offers this part-time programme to those working in related fields who cannot attend full-time postgraduate studies. Classes are held in the evening after 5pm.

Programme Structure

The MS programme consists of 30 credit hours. Students may choose from the following options:

  • 24 credit hours of coursework and 6 credit hours for Project/Thesis
  • 30 credit hours of coursework only

Compulsory Courses

  • DSC-605: Programming for Data Science

    Problem solving for data science, programming constructs, data structures, data wrangling, introduction to data analytics, and visualization basics.

  • DSC-606: Probability and Statistics for Data Science

    Probability spaces, random variables, multivariate random variables, hypothesis testing, Bayesian methods, linear regression, applications in data science.

Elective Courses

  • DSC-632: Advanced Web Development

    Essentials of web development including JavaScript, synchronous and asynchronous programming, server-side scripting, API development, and databases.

  • DSC-653: Natural Language Processing

    Introduction to NLP, regular expressions, text classification, sentiment analysis, information retrieval, question answering, and dialogue systems.

  • DSC-660: Research Methods

    Introduces research methodologies and techniques used in data science research, including literature review, hypothesis testing, and research design.

  • DSC-665: Cloud Computing

    Explores cloud computing technologies, architectures, services, and implementation strategies, including SaaS, PaaS, and IaaS models.

  • DSC-672: e-Government

    Examines the role of technology in public administration and the development of digital government services for citizens.

  • DSC-673: Multimedia Technology

    Focuses on multimedia systems and the processing and analysis of digital images, video, and audio within the context of data science.

  • DSC-675: Information Retrieval Systems

    Introduces information retrieval concepts including indexing, ranking algorithms, and systems for text-based search and recommendation.

  • DSC-731: Optimization Methods for Data Science

    Studies optimization techniques and algorithms used in machine learning and data science, including gradient descent and genetic algorithms.

  • DSC-741: Computer Vision

    Covers image processing, pattern recognition, object detection, and feature extraction techniques used in computer vision applications.

  • DSC-742: Internet of Things

    Explores the integration of IoT devices with networks and applications, and how data is collected, analyzed, and utilized for real-time decision-making.

  • DSC-772: Data Mining

    Focuses on techniques and algorithms for extracting useful patterns and knowledge from large datasets using data mining tools.

  • DSC-775: Digital Libraries

    Examines the design, development, and management of digital libraries, including metadata standards, content management, and access systems.

  • DSC-781: Content-based Information Retrieval

    Explores content-based retrieval techniques for multimedia, focusing on image, audio, and video data search and analysis methods.

  • DSC-787: Special Topics in Data Science

    A seminar-style course focused on emerging trends, technologies, and challenges in the field of data science, including guest lectures and case studies.

  • DSC-815: Neural Information Retrieval

    An advanced course focusing on the application of deep learning models for information retrieval, including neural networks and natural language processing.

  • DSC-816: Medical Image Analysis

    Teaches techniques for analyzing medical images using computer vision and machine learning algorithms to detect and classify diseases.

  • DSC-817: Transfer Learning and Applications

    Focuses on the application of transfer learning techniques, allowing models trained on one task to be adapted to new tasks with minimal data.

  • DSC-818: Advanced Computer Vision

    An in-depth study of advanced computer vision techniques including 3D vision, deep learning architectures, and real-time object tracking.

  • DSC-819: Machine Learning for Software Engineering

    Application of machine learning methods to software engineering, including bug prediction, performance optimization, and automated testing.

  • DSC-823: Data Warehousing

    Covers the design, development, and deployment of data warehousing systems, including ETL processes and data integration techniques.

  • DSC-825: Machine Learning

    Introduces supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms, including decision trees, SVM, clustering, and neural networks.

  • DSC-826: Pattern Recognition

    Focuses on techniques for classifying and identifying patterns in data, including algorithms like k-means, nearest neighbor, and hidden Markov models.

  • DSC-827: Social Network Analysis

    Studies methods for analyzing social networks, including graph theory, community detection, and influence propagation models.

  • DSC-828: Computational Modeling

    Introduces computational techniques for modeling complex systems, including simulation, optimization, and agent-based modeling.

  • DSC-829: Social Media Mining

    Explores methods and algorithms for extracting useful insights from social media platforms, including sentiment analysis and trend detection.

  • DSC-8XX: Advanced Social Network Analysis

    A deep dive into advanced topics in social network analysis, including network centrality, influence maximization, and viral spread.

  • DSC-838: Recommender Systems

    An introduction to building recommendation engines, including collaborative filtering, content-based filtering, and hybrid methods.

  • DSC-839: Information Cryptography

    Explores cryptographic techniques used for securing information, including encryption algorithms, hashing, and public key infrastructure.
