MPhil in Computer Science Overview

The objective of the M.Phil Computer Science program is to train students to initiate research work. It lasts two years (4 semesters) with 50 credit hours, including 24 credits for coursework and 26 for research. The program emphasizes research work and aims to build a foundation for advanced studies.

CSC-652: Advanced Analysis of Algorithms

An in-depth study of algorithm analysis, including dynamic programming, graph algorithms, encryption algorithms, and NP-completeness.

CSC-702: Advanced Operating Systems

Covers operating systems' fundamentals with a focus on distributed systems, fault tolerance, resource management, and scalable server design.

Elective Courses

  • CSC-601: Theory of Computation

    Models of computation, Turing machines, Chomsky hierarchy, decidability, and resource-bounded computational complexity.

  • CSC-604: Advanced Software Engineering

    Focuses on engineering principles, software project management, verification, validation, and cost-effective software design.

  • CSC-611: Human and Information Interaction

    Studies human interactions with information systems, information retrieval, behavior, usability, and search techniques.

  • CSC-614: Software Quality Assurance and Testing

    Focuses on software testing methods, quality assurance processes, and methodologies for effective software testing and validation.

  • CSC-624: Requirements Engineering

    Covers the principles and techniques used for gathering, analyzing, and specifying software requirements.

  • CSC-631: Web Services

    Teaches the design, implementation, and deployment of web services using industry-standard protocols and technologies.

  • CSC-632: Advanced Web Development

    Explores advanced web technologies, frameworks, and the development of scalable and efficient web applications.

  • CSC-650: Web Science

    Studies the interdisciplinary study of web technologies and the web's impact on society, economics, and information systems.

  • CSC-651: Advanced Computer Architecture

    Focuses on the design and performance analysis of modern computer systems and architectures.

  • CSC-65X: Intelligent Information System

    Explores artificial intelligence techniques, including machine learning, natural language processing, and expert systems.

  • CSC-653: Natural Language Processing

    Covers computational approaches to processing and understanding human language, including syntactic, semantic, and discourse analysis.

  • CSC-654: Disciplined Software Processes

    Introduces disciplined approaches to software engineering, emphasizing structured processes and methodologies.

  • CSC-660: Research Methods

    Teaches essential research methodologies and techniques used in computer science research, including hypothesis testing and data analysis.

  • CSC-664: Knowledge Management for Software Engineering

    Focuses on techniques and tools for managing knowledge in software engineering, including knowledge representation and organizational knowledge management.

  • CSC-665: Cloud Computing

    An introduction to cloud computing technologies, services, and architecture, including the use of cloud-based infrastructures and services.

  • CSC-667: Wireless Sensor Networks

    Covers the design, deployment, and management of wireless sensor networks for various applications, including monitoring and data collection.

  • CSC-668: Advanced Computer Networks

    Explores advanced topics in computer networking, including network protocols, security, and performance optimization.

  • CSC-671: Theory of Programming Languages

    Covers the theory behind programming languages, including syntax, semantics, and language design.

  • CSC-672: Data Compression

    Focuses on techniques for compressing and decompressing data in various formats, including lossless and lossy compression methods.

  • CSC-673: Multimedia Technology

    An introduction to multimedia technologies, including audio, video, graphics, and interactive media.

  • CSC-674: Advanced Human-Computer Interaction

    Covers advanced principles in human-computer interaction design, focusing on usability and user experience.

  • CSC-675: Information Retrieval Systems

    Teaches information retrieval models and algorithms, including indexing, searching, and ranking of information.

  • CSC-677: World Wide Web and Society

    Examines the impact of the World Wide Web on society, culture, and communication, including ethical and legal considerations.

  • CSC-714: Software Architectures

    Explores software architecture design and analysis, focusing on patterns, scalability, and maintainability.

  • CSC-724: Software Patterns

    Focuses on software design patterns, teaching the use of proven solutions to recurring design problems in software development.
